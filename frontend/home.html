Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about IPOs and investing

What is an IPO? +
An IPO (Initial Public Offering) is when a private company offers shares to the public for the first time, allowing it to raise capital from public investors.
Who can apply for an IPO? +
Any Indian citizen with a valid PAN card, a Demat account, and a trading account with a registered broker can apply for an IPO.
What is a price band? +
The price band is the range within which investors can bid for shares in a book-building IPO. The final price is determined based on investor demand.
What is a Demat account? +
A Demat account holds your shares in electronic format, similar to how a bank account holds money. It's required to hold securities when trading in the stock market.
Can I cancel my IPO application? +
Yes, you can modify or withdraw your IPO application before the issue closing date through your broker or bank.
What is the minimum amount to invest in an IPO? +
The minimum investment is determined by the lot size and price band. Typically, it ranges from ₹14,000 to ₹15,000 for retail investors.
